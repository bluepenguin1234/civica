Civica Score (0–100)

Each pillar is scored 0–100 based on available data. Pillar scores are multiplied by their weight and summed to produce the composite Civica Score. When data is missing, pillar scores are estimated conservatively and the town's confidence rating is adjusted accordingly.

Tax Efficiency Ratio (TER) — Civica Proprietary Metric

The TER measures what residents get for what they pay. A town can have a high tax bill but deliver exceptional schools, safety, and infrastructure — making it high value. Conversely, a low-tax town with failing schools and poor services scores poorly on TER.

TER = (School Score × 0.40) + (Safety Score × 0.25) + (Infrastructure Score × 0.20) + (Fiscal Score × 0.15) ÷ Normalized Tax Burden

Our editorial principles

  • We cite every data point. If a source isn't verifiable, we mark the field as pending rather than estimate.
  • We flag data gaps explicitly and adjust the confidence rating. A score is only as trustworthy as the data behind it.
  • We do not accept payment from towns, real estate agencies, or developers to adjust scores.
  • Scores are updated when material new data is published — not on a fixed calendar.
  • The Honest Buyer Take is written from the buyer's perspective, not the town's. We include red flags even for high-scoring towns.
  • When a bond rating changes due to a methodology shift rather than the town's fundamentals, we note it in the compiler notes.
  • Civica scores reflect municipal fiscal, academic, and governance data only. The racial or demographic composition of a town's residents is not a scoring input and is not displayed. Civica is committed to fair housing principles under the Fair Housing Act (42 U.S.C. § 3604).

Data confidence ratings

HighAll major fiscal, school, and safety data points are sourced from primary records. Score is stable.
MediumA few fields estimated or sourced from secondary data. Score is directionally reliable but may shift by ±3–5 points.
LowMultiple key fields are pending primary source verification. Score should be treated as preliminary.

Data freshness policy

CurrentData verified within the last 3 years. Displayed normally.
FlaggedData last verified 3–5 years ago. Shown with an "unverified since [year]" note so you know its age.
ExcludedData older than 5 years is removed from the profile and excluded from scoring entirely. A bond rating from 2018, for example, tells you little about a town's current fiscal standing.

Primary data sources: Census ACS 2023 five-year estimates · DESE 2025–26 district profiles · S&P and Moody's bond ratings (current where verified) · FBI UCR / MA EOPSS crime data · MA PERAC pension reports.
