Recommended First Moves

1
Sell agent slots per town, not globally An agent who specializes in Marblehead or Andover will pay a premium to own that one profile exclusively. Pitch it as "own this page" — not just a banner ad.
2
Land a mortgage lender first Easiest cold pitch — lenders buy anything buyer-facing. One regional lender (think Rockland Trust, Eastern Bank, Cape Ann Savings) could buy all 36 calculator placements as a package deal.
3
Add a "Claim this page" CTA to every profile A small "Are you an agent in [Town]? Advertise here →" link at the bottom of every profile generates inbound leads passively. Build the waitlist before you cold-pitch.
4
Create an Advertise page A simple one-page rate card at civica.com/advertise lets agents and vendors find you. Include slot types, pricing tiers, and a contact form.
Key advantage over Zillow/Realtor.com: Civica reaches buyers at the pre-search stage — before they have a saved search, before they've picked a town. That's earlier in the funnel than any listing platform, which means your audience is more open to influence. That's a story worth telling advertisers.
