Methodology
How Flags Are Calculated
Each flag uses public data and objective thresholds. No editorial judgment is applied to individual municipalities. Thresholds are based on MA state guidance, actuarial standards, and peer comparisons within the 36-town dataset.
1
Pension Funding Level
Source: MA PERAC annual actuarial valuations. Most Essex County towns participate in the Essex Regional Retirement System (~53.8% funded). Cities with independent systems (Lynn, Lawrence, Haverhill, Methuen) are valued separately.

Thresholds: Critical <55% funded · Warning 55–70%

The 70% floor reflects common actuarial guidance. Below 55%, unfunded liabilities typically require significant future tax increases to resolve.
Coverage: 36/36 towns (100%)
2
Free Cash Reserves
Source: MA Division of Local Services (DLS) certified free cash — the prior year's operating surplus after all liabilities are settled, certified by the state each fall.

Thresholds: Critical <2% · Warning 2–3%

MA DLS recommends municipalities maintain at least 5% free cash as a fiscal buffer. Below 3% signals limited capacity to absorb unexpected expenses or revenue shortfalls without borrowing.
Coverage: ~22/36 towns (61%)
3
Academic Return on Investment
Compares each town's per-pupil spending percentile rank (among 36 peers) against its academic outcome percentile (DESE district ranking, 351 districts). Formula: gap = spend_pct − outcome_pct.

Thresholds: Critical gap >30 · Warning gap 15–30

A positive gap means a town spends more than peers yet achieves lower outcomes. This is a signal — not proof of mismanagement — as district size, demographics, and special education ratios all affect per-pupil spending.
Coverage: 34/36 towns
4
Per-Capita Debt Load
Source: Town ACFRs (Annual Comprehensive Financial Reports) and MA DLS gateway. Includes general obligation bonds; excludes revenue bonds and BANs unless noted.

Thresholds: Critical >$7,000/resident · Warning $4,500–$7,000

The Massachusetts median is approximately $2,400 per capita. High debt/capita is not inherently negative — it can reflect deliberate capital investment — but it constrains future budget flexibility and raises debt service costs.
Coverage: ~12/36 towns (33%)
Data Sources
Where the Numbers Come From
All data is derived from public records. Confidence levels for each municipality are shown in the main Civica scorecard.
MA PERAC
Pension funding ratios — annual actuarial valuations for all public pension systems
MA DLS Gateway
Certified free cash, debt schedules, and municipal finance data
MA DESE
District rankings, per-pupil spending, graduation rates, MCAS math scores
EMMA / MSRB
Bond ratings and official municipal disclosure filings
FBI UCR / MA EOPSS
Violent and property crime rates per 100,000 residents
Census ACS 2023
Population, income, poverty, education attainment, demographics
Limitation Notice: Civica flags are statistical screening tools, not audit findings. A flag does not indicate fraud, waste, or abuse — it identifies a data point that deviates from peer averages or established thresholds and warrants public attention. Many flags reflect structural factors (regional pension systems, small-district scale effects, capital investment cycles) rather than management failures. Some data is estimated or incomplete where primary sources were inaccessible; confidence levels are documented in the main Civica scorecard. This tool is for informational purposes only. Civica is not affiliated with any government entity.
