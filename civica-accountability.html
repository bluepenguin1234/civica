Methodology
How Flags Are Calculated
Every flag uses public data and objective thresholds. No editorial judgment is applied to individual municipalities. Thresholds are based on MA state guidance, actuarial standards, and peer analysis within the 36-town dataset.
🏦
Pension Funding · Fiscal Health
Source: MA PERAC annual actuarial reports. Most Essex towns share the Essex Regional Retirement System (~53.8% funded). Cities with independent systems (Lynn, Lawrence, Haverhill, Methuen) are separately valued.

Critical below 55% · Warning 55–70%

Below 55%, unfunded liabilities typically force large future tax increases to keep the system solvent.
Coverage: 36 / 36 towns (100%)
💵
Free Cash Reserves · Fiscal Health
Source: MA Division of Local Services (DLS) certified free cash — prior year's operating surplus after all liabilities, certified by the state each fall.

Critical below 2% · Warning 2–3%

MA DLS recommends 5%+ as a minimum buffer. Below 3% means almost no cushion for unexpected costs or revenue shortfalls without short-term borrowing.
Coverage: ~22 / 36 towns (61%)
🎓
Academic ROI Gap · Schools
Compares each town's per-pupil spending percentile vs. its school rank percentile (DESE, 351 districts) within the 36-town peer group. Formula: gap = spend_pct − outcome_pct.

Critical gap >30 pts · Warning gap 15–30 pts

A large gap signals above-peer spending with below-peer outcomes. District size and demographics also affect per-pupil costs — context matters.
Coverage: 34 / 36 towns
📊
Per-Capita Debt Load · Fiscal Health
Source: Town ACFRs and MA DLS gateway. Includes general obligation bonds. High debt/capita can reflect capital investment (schools, roads) — not inherently negative — but raises annual debt service costs and limits future budget flexibility.

Critical above $7,000 · Warning $4,500–$7,000

MA median ≈ $2,400/capita.
Coverage: ~12 / 36 towns (33%)
🚨
Violent Crime Rate · Safety
Source: FBI UCR / MA EOPSS. Violent crimes (murder, rape, robbery, aggravated assault) per 100,000 residents. Essex County average ≈ 108/100k.

Critical above 450/100k · Warning above 250/100k

High crime rates directly affect quality of life and property values, and may indicate under-investment in public safety resources relative to population.
Coverage: 31 / 36 towns
📉
School District Rank · Schools
Source: DESE district profiles / SchoolDigger. Rank among all 351 Massachusetts school districts based on MCAS performance, graduation rate, and academic growth.

Critical bottom 10% (rank >315) · Warning bottom 20% (rank >280)

Low rank does not always reflect mismanagement — high poverty and ELL populations drive outcomes — but it does affect property values and buyer decisions.
Coverage: 34 / 36 towns
💧
Drinking Water Violations · Infrastructure
Source: EPA ECHO (Enforcement and Compliance History Online) / Safe Drinking Water Information System. Counts health-based violations reported within a 5-year window.

Warning any violation in 5-year window

Even one health-based violation warrants resident attention and direct follow-up with the municipal water department.
Coverage: 36 / 36 towns
📋
Data Transparency · All Pillars
Counts how many of Civica's 67 tracked data fields are unavailable or unverified for a given municipality. Towns with 20+ gaps cannot be fully screened by any of the above flags.

Info flag when gaps ≥ 20

Limited data often reflects small staff capacity or deliberate non-disclosure. Residents can file a public records request for ACFR and budget documents.
Coverage: 36 / 36 towns
Data Sources
Where the Numbers Come From
All data sourced from public records. Confidence levels for each municipality are shown in the main Civica scorecard.
MA PERAC
Annual actuarial valuations for all public pension systems
MA DLS Gateway
Certified free cash, debt schedules, municipal finance
MA DESE
District rankings, per-pupil spending, MCAS, grad rates
EMMA / MSRB
Bond ratings and official municipal disclosures
FBI UCR / MA EOPSS
Violent and property crime rates per 100k residents
Census ACS 2023
Population, income, poverty, education attainment
Important: Civica flags are statistical screening tools, not audit findings. A flag does not indicate fraud, waste, or abuse — it identifies a data point that deviates from peer averages or established thresholds and warrants public attention. Many flags reflect structural factors (regional pension systems, small-district scale effects, capital investment cycles) rather than management failures. Some data is estimated where primary sources were inaccessible. This tool is for informational purposes only. Civica is not affiliated with any government entity.
